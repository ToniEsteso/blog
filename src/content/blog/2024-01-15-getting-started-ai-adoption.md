The gap between "it works in the notebook" and "it runs reliably in production" is where most AI projects go to die.

## The Reality

Your data science team built a great model. The accuracy metrics look good. The stakeholders are excited. Then someone asks: "How do we actually use this in our system?"

That's where things get interesting.

## What Actually Matters

1. **Reliability over accuracy** - A model that's always wrong is predictable. A model that's usually right but occasionally catastrophic is a liability.

2. **Monitoring from day one** - You can't improve what you don't measure. Set up observability before deployment.

3. **Edge cases are features** - Every exception is an opportunity to make your system more robust.

## The Takeaway

Ship early. Monitor everything. Iterate constantly.
